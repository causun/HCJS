Homemade Yogurt

            

                

                    Total:
                    15 mins
                

                

                    Prep:
                    10 mins
                

                

                    Cook:
                    5 mins
                

                

                    Waiting time:
                    8 hrs
                

                

                    Vield:
                    1 quart(4 servings)

Yogurt is fairly easy to make at home and the result is much better in flavor and consistency than most commercial Yogurt. You will save money making your own,
and also be able to choose what does or doesn't go into your Yogurt (commercial brands often include thickeners and other non-dairy ingredients).

Ingredients

  • 1 quart/1 liter milk
  • 2 tablespoons Yogurt (with active cultures: all commercial breands will state on the container if the Yogurt contains active cultures.

Steps to Make it

  1. Gently heat the milk over mediun low heat until it reaches 180 oF/82oC. Turn off the head and the milk cool down to between 106oF/41oC and 110oC/43oC.
  2. While the heated milk is cooling, wask the jars and leave them filled with hot water so the glass will be warm when you add the Yogurt
  3. When the milk has cooled enough, warm a large bowl or measuring cup by filling it with hot water. Empty out the the water. Strain yhe milk through a fime smesh strainer into the warmed bowl. Whisk in the yogurt. Pour out the water in the jars and pour in the milk plus yogurt mixture. Fasten the lids.
  4. Place the jars in a warm but not hot place where they can remain undisturbed for 8 hours or overnight. An old fashioned oven in which the pilot light is always on is perfect. so is an oven with the light turned on but the heat off. Another option is a food detrydrator set to 110oC/43oC with the trays removed to
    to make room for the jars. Or you could buy a special yogurt maker that maintains the ideal temperature.
  5. Be careful not to jostle the jars while the active yogurt cultures are working on the milk. Yogurt is very finicky about this, and will sometimes not set up well
    if the jars are moved during this period
  6. Trasfer the jars to the refrigerator.
